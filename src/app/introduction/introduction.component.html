Introduction

The Civil Engineering Association (CEA) at IIT Bombay was established with the primary goal of disseminating knowledge and addressing industrial issues by bringing corporates, professors, and students together on a common platform. CEA, including both students and faculty members, is one of the most active civil engineering organizations in the country.
We aim to promote Civil Engineering by giving community members much-needed practical exposure through regular activities such as technical seminars, research symposiums, talks on ongoing research practices around the world, and many other related topics from distinguished practitioners of the trade. Collaboration between the university and industry is critical for the advancement of engineering education and research. In order to provide our students with practical experience in Civil Engineering, we organize several visits to ongoing construction sites throughout the year, allowing them to interact with key industry personnel.
Aside from technical aspects, we also take on the responsibility of proper student nurturing by organizing social events as part of extracurricular activities. Some of the events organized by CEA throughout the year include "Freshers Day" as a break-the-ice event for newcomers, intra-departmental sports and cultural events at the UG and PG levels, department treks and trips, and so on.
Aakaar, the annual festival of the Civil Engineering Department at IIT Bombay, is organized by the student body of the Civil Engineering Association at IIT Bombay. Aakaar gives budding civil engineers across the country a platform to create, innovate, and learn about various aspects of civil engineering through competitions, workshops, panel discussions, and symposiums (research paper conference). Aakaar has since gone on to become the undisputed leader in civil engineering festivals.
CEA Student Bodies are also involved in an international civil engineering organisation called 'EERI.' Since 1948, the Earthquake Engineering Research Institute (EERI) has been the leading non-profit membership organisation connecting professionals worldwide dedicated to reducing earthquake risk. By establishing the EERI IIT Bombay student chapter in March 2021, IIT Bombay became a part of this legacy. This chapter aims to provide information about earthquakes and their social, economic, and environmental consequences, as well as practises for reducing earthquake risk. This chapter encourages students to pursue careers in earthquake engineering and related fields. The EERI Student Chapter at IIT Bombay provides a venue for students to participate in a variety of EERI competitions and activities.
