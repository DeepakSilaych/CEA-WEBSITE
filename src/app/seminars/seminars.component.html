02nd January

Next-Generation Risk-Targeted Design Standards


Speaker: Dr. Prakash Singh Badal
ABSTRACT:
Economic losses from moderate earthquake events centered close to a metropolis can potentially wreak havoc on local economies and small countries. Recent studies suggest that every dollar spent on a resilient infrastructure translates to $13 in avoided losses. And yet, seismic losses have not created the same political or economic imperative as other risks, such as diseases or financial crises. In this presentation, we will discuss different ways to bridge the paramount gap between the state-of-the-art knowledge of performance-based earthquake engineering and the development of next-generation seismic design standards. Twofold issues arise in this quest. First is the trade-off between the rigors expected from a structural engineer and the predictable performance of the built infrastructure. The second issue is that of setting the target performance itself. With the help of extensive studies on two different lateral load-resisting systems, we will discuss the proposed calibration of seismic design factors to meet probabilistic performance targets. We then address ways to set the target performance. We will conclude the presentation with a discussion on the ongoing thrusts of the global earthquake engineering community to achieve resilient infrastructure.
About the Speaker:
Dr. Prakash Singh Badal is a postdoctoral researcher at the University of California, Davis working with Prof. Michele Barbato. Prior to this, he worked as a postdoc with Prof. Solomon Tesfamariam at the University of British Columbia, where he developed high-rise hybrid building systems and created tools for ground motion selection per the latest NBCC 2020. Dr. Badal obtained his Ph.D. from IIT Bombay, working with Prof. Ravi Sinha, where he proposed a framework for multi-objective performance-based seismic design. Upon completing his Ph.D., he was conferred the Institute’s Shankar Dayal Sharma Gold medal. He has an avid interest in optimization, where he has developed two state-of-the-art heuristic algorithms to solve a class of NP-hard problems. He loves long strenuous hikes and is an aspiring Squash player.

ABSTRACT:

Role of Artificial Intelligence and Probabilistic Analytics in Earthquake Engineering


Speaker: Prof. Abhinav Gupta
ABSTRACT:
"The field of Earthquake Engineering has grown many folds over the past several decades. Initially, the primary objective of earthquake resistant design was focused only on life safety. Over the past decade, there has been an increasing demand for not only life safety but also minimizing the property loss as well as minimizing the disruption of operations at various facilities that are essential for a quick recovery from a natural disaster. Such a demand has increased the need for significant improvement in our ability to simulate the linear and nonlinear behavior of structures and industrial systems. It has also increased the need for improvement in our ability to simulate ageing related degradation and damage in structures and components. Large array of sensors are being used to collect data and it is expected to assist us in detecting possible degradation and damage in structures as well as possibly predict when a potential failure might occur. However, we are very far from achieving this goal. In fact, researchers have not been able to fully simulate the exact behavior of complex structures when subjected to large earthquake inputs in a highly controlled environment of a shake table test. For example, simulating the nonlinear behavior of complex concrete structures and establishing an appropriate constitutive model for the nonlinear damage plasticity by itself is quite challenging. There are significant uncertainties that remain in our ability to formulate and simulate the behavior of such complex systems throughout their lifetime. Deploying greater number of sensors, collecting big data, and using modern techniques like Artificial Intelligence by themselves are not likely to provide the solution to the current challenges. Yet, artificial intelligence and probabilistic analytics can provide us better insights into the behavior of complex structural systems. It can eventually help in improving the characterization of constitutive behavior and degradation mechanisms. This seminar will present the results from recent research conducted in this field at the Center for Nuclear Energy Facilities and Structures (CNEFS). An open session would be conducted after the lecture aimed at UG and PG students who might be interested in pursuing higher studies in US."

17th October

Recent Advances in Nonlinear Seismic Soil-Structure Interaction: Raft, Piles and CPRF for NPP


Speaker: Prof. B. K. Maheswari
ABSTRACT:
"Soil-Structure Interaction (SSI) is one of the design issues, which cannot be ignored for the structures founded on the weak soils particularly when these are subjected to strong excitation. During strong shaking, the behavior of soil is highly nonlinear; further if it is saturated loose sand, the liquefaction may occur. To deal effectively with such complex scenario, numerical modeling provides the best solution. For Soil-Structure Interaction considering earthquake loads, there are a number of important issues which need to be dealt rationally for a reasonably accurate analysis. First issue is modeling of unbounded soil media to satisfy the radiation conditions. Second, for dynamic loading, behavior of soil is frequency dependent and it needs to be taken care. Third is modeling material nonlinearity of soil. Fourth is modeling of liquefaction. To deal with boundary, both simplified and rigorous methods are available. Some of the new upcoming nuclear power plants (NPP) in India are proposed to be founded on the soft soil i.e. alluvium in active seismic zone, necessitating to consider SSI for analysis. Few of these will be founded on Combined Piled-Raft Foundation (CPRF). So far, no study has been reported considering nonlinearity of soil for the NPP structures founded on CPRF considering earthquake loads. The speaker and his research group examined the dynamic responses of raft, pile group and CPRF of a NPP simulating the nonlinear finite element model. The effect of nonlinearity is examined. The study indicated the importance of CPRF over the raft foundation and pile group foundation for the design of nuclear power plants."
About the Speaker:
Dr. B.K. Maheshwari is currently serving as a Shamsher Prakash Chair Professor at Dept. of Earthquake Engineering, IIT Roorkee. He secured Ph.D. from Saitama University Japan then worked for about two years in industry in Tokyo and then joined Washington University in St. Louis, Missouri, USA as a post-doctoral fellow. The connection between Southern Ocean winds, Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation and Indo-Pacific upwelling.


Speaker: Dr. Markus Jochum
ABSTRACT:
Coupled climate model simulations are analyzed to quantify the dynamic effect of Southern Ocean (SO) winds on transports in the oceans. It is found that the representation of ocean mesoscale eddies does not allow for a realistic eddy saturation of the zonal transports in the SO in response to the wind changes, and that eddy compensation of the meridional transports in the SO is underestimated too. Despite this underestimated eddy compensation in the SO, however, and in contrast to previous suggestions, the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) strength is almost insensitive to SO winds. In the limit of weak SO winds the AMOC waters do not upwell in the SO but in the tropical Indo-Pacific. Through their effect on sea-ice, weaker SO winds also lead to less production of Antarctic Bottom Water and therefore a deeper and stronger AMOC.
